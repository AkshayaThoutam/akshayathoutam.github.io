About Me

Hello! I am Akshaya Thoutam, a Pre-doctoral Research Assistant with the Biomedical Machine Learning group at Microsoft Research in Cambridge, Massachusetts. I graduated from the Georgia Institute of Technology as a College of Sciences Dean's Scholar and Early Research Award recipient with a B.S in Neuroscience and a minor in health and medical sciences. My advisors have included Greg Gibson (GT), Nasrin Hooshmand (GT), Simon Knott (Cedars Sinai), and Joshua Levin (Broad).

Currently co-advised by Lorin Crawford and Ava Amini at Microsoft Research, I am part of a joint collaboration between Microsoft and the Broad Institute - Project Ex Vivo. My research focuses on the manipulation of single cell foundation models to better understand cell-state level biology. Broadly, I am interested in the use of multimodal machine learning and systems biology approaches to advance our understanding of healthy and diseased state models. I am currently auditing the following courses at MIT/Harvard: (1) HST.507J Advanced Computational Biology: Genomes, Networks, Evolution and (2) HST.525J Tumor Microenvironment and Immuno-Oncology
