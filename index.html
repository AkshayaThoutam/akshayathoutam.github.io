About Me

Hello! I am a Pre-doctoral Research Assistant with the Biomedical Machine Learning group at Microsoft Research in Cambridge, Massachusetts. I graduated from the Georgia Institute of Technology as a College of Sciences Deans Scholar and Early Research Award recipient with a B.S in Neuroscience and a minor in health and medical sciences. I have had the privilege of being advised by Greg Gibson (Georgia Tech), Nasrin Hooshmand (Georgia Tech), Simon Knott (Cedars Sinai), and Joshua Levin (Broad).

I am currently co-advised by Lorin Crawford and Ava Amini at Microsoft Research. I am part of a joint collaboration between Microsoft and the Broad Institute - Project Ex Vivo. My research focuses on the manipulation of single cell foundation models to better understand cell-state level biology.

Other Areas of Research That Excite Me

  • Cancer genomics and Precision Oncology: Next-generation sequencing (NGS) is used to analyze the molecular makeup of tumors in order to help diagnose and treat cancer. Current uses can provide targetable genetic alterations to increase efficacy of treatment. By integrating single-cell information we create treatment paradigms that are better targeted to an individual's specific tumor microenvironment.
  • Complexity and Data: Atlases provide a comprehensive reference framework that integrates data from multiple sources, allowing for a more robust analysis and interpretation.
  • Neuroscience: The human brain is multifaceted and inherently heterogeneous, consisting of a diverse ensemble of cellular and molecular components that synergize to produce our thoughts, emotions, and behavior. Multimodal models can paint us a more coherent picture of this dynamic. Multiomic data can provide information about the gene regulatory networks that contribute to molecular processes. Electrophysiology shows how these molecular processes manifest in the functional behavior of individual neurons and neural circuits. Molecular dynamics define how these molecular mechanisms are governed by general physics models and chemical principles.
